Materials that Matter

Choices and Criteria of Materials

Brass Alloy: This metal is valued for its durability and tarnish resistance, making it ideal for long-lasting jewellery that withstands daily wear while maintaining its shine.

Stainless Steel: Known for being skin-friendly, stainless steel is perfect for wearers with sensitive skin, offering comfort and resistance to corrosion for everyday use.

Enamel: Applied to jewellery, enamel provides a vibrant artistic finish, adding bold colors and creative designs that enhance aesthetic appeal without increasing weight.

Cubic Zirconia: This material brings sparkle and a luxury-like appeal to Salty’s pieces, mimicking the brilliance of diamonds at an affordable price point.

Metals for Every Mood

Which metals does Salty prefer for everyday versus premium pieces?

Salty Jewellery prefers different metals based on the intended wear and price positioning of the pieces:

Everyday Pieces: Salty primarily uses brass alloy, copper alloy, and stainless steel for their everyday jewellery. These metals offer good durability, tarnish resistance, and are skin-friendly, making them suitable for frequent use and affordable price points. Brass and copper alloys form the base for most casual and trendy designs.

Premium Pieces: For higher-end or “premium” collections, Salty uses electroplated metals with 14K or 18K gold plating as well as some sterling silver elements to give a more luxurious finish and durability. These materials elevate the look and feel for special occasion pieces or more refined designs while maintaining affordability.

This approach allows Salty to maintain its core promise of affordable luxury by balancing material cost, durability, and aesthetics to suit varied customer needs and occasions.
